# OmegAmino Audience Profile

## Primary Audience

**Research-literate fitness enthusiasts** who self-direct their supplementation and recovery protocols. They read studies. They track their stacks. They distrust hype. They respond to specificity.

Sub-segments within this audience:
- **Peptide users** — BPC-157, TB-500, GLP-1/semaglutide compound users
- **Biohackers** — protocol-driven, data-oriented, interested in optimization
- **Performance athletes** — recovery, body composition, performance focus
- **Medical-adjacent** — familiar with mechanism language, skeptical of marketing claims

---

## How This Audience Thinks

| What they value | What they reject |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Mechanism-level explanations | Vague benefit claims ("feel better") |
| Protocol specificity | Before/after hype |
| Third-party validation | Brand-authored authority claims |
| Peer recommendations | Hard sells |
| Efficiency of information | Long-form education dumps |

**Key insight:** This audience will click on a link that opens a knowledge gap. They will not click on a link that just offers a discount.

---

## Copy Approach by Audience State

### Cold / New Subscribers
- Lead with credibility signal (compound name, protocol context, or data point)
- Do not lead with offer or discount
- Earn trust before asking for anything

### Engaged Subscribers
- Peer voice, assume prior knowledge
- Skip the "what is BPC-157" setup — they know
- Go straight to the angle (new protocol, stack context, product update)

### Lapsed / Re-engagement
- Acknowledge the gap simply, without drama
- Lead with something new or useful
- No guilt or urgency pressure
