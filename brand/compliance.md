# OmegAmino Copy Compliance Rules

Every draft for OmegAmino email and SMS must pass this check before delivery.

---

## FDA / FTC Standing Constraints

OmegAmino sells research compounds and supplements. Marketing claims are subject to FDA and FTC scrutiny.

**Standing rules:**
- No disease claims (do not claim a product treats, cures, or prevents any condition)
- No outcome guarantees ("you will lose X lbs", "guaranteed recovery")
- Mechanism language drifts fast — every draft requires a compliance pass before send
- GLP-1 / semaglutide compounds carry the **highest regulatory risk** — apply extra scrutiny
- Melanotan 2 is **off-limits** for all campaign content

**Safe framing:**
- "Studied for..." / "Research suggests..." / "Protocol-focused..."
- Community/peer framing ("how athletes are using...")
- Outcome-agnostic benefit language ("supports recovery", "part of a stack")

**Unsafe framing (rewrite immediately):**
- "Heals" / "cures" / "treats" / "reverses"
- "Clinically proven to..."
- "FDA approved" (unless literally true for that product)
- Weight loss outcome guarantees for GLP-1 compounds

## Style Compliance (Voice Rules)

These are enforced on every draft regardless of channel:

**Never use:**
| Phrase / Pattern | Why |
|-----------------|-----|
| Em dash (—) as stylistic device | Chase preference, non-negotiable |
| "Changes everything" / "shifts everything" / "transforms everything" | AI-sounding hype |
| "Nobody says out loud" | Overused, inauthentic |
| "Leveraging" / "leverage" | Corporate buzzword |
| "Deep dive" | Overused |
| "Moving the needle" | Corporate buzzword |
| "At the end of the day" | Filler |
| "Game-changer" | Hype, not credible |
| "Synergy" / "innovative" / "seamlessly" | Corporate buzzword |
| "Unlock" / "elevate" | Marketing cliché |
