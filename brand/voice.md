# OmegAmino Brand Voice

## Brand Character

OmegAmino is a performance-focused supplement brand for serious, research-driven athletes. The brand does not talk down to its audience — it talks *with* them. The voice is peer-level: knowledgeable, direct, and science-literate without being clinical or academic.

**One-line test:** Would a smart, skeptical 30-year-old who reads PubMed feel respected by this copy? If not, rewrite it.

---

## Tone Dimensions

| Dimension | OmegAmino IS | OmegAmino IS NOT |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Knowledge | Science-literate, protocol-aware | Clinical, academic, jargon-heavy |
| Energy | Focused, purposeful | Hype-driven, loud |
| Relationship | Peer, collaborator | Authority, advisor, teacher |
| Length | Short, dense with meaning | Long-winded, padded |
| Personality | Confident, direct | Arrogant, pushy |

---

## Chase Voice Rules (Client-Facing Copy)

These rules apply to all copy written in Chase's voice or for Chase's channels.

**Non-negotiable:**
1. Casual, peer-level — not a consultant delivering a report
2. Short sentences. Direct. No corporate preamble.
3. Ideas lead with the recommendation, not the rationale
4. One clarifying sentence max after the point — never over-explain
5. No em dashes (—) as stylistic devices

**Never use these phrases:**
- "Leveraging" / "leverage"
- "Deep dive"
- "Moving the needle"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Changes everything" / "shifts everything" / "transforms everything"
- "Nobody says out loud"
- "Game-changer"
- "Synergy" / "innovative" / "seamlessly"
- "Unlock" / "elevate"

## Email Copy Voice

**Audience:** Research-literate, skeptical, protocol-driven peptide users.

**Approach:**
- First-person, direct
- Give readers just enough to drive a click — not a full education
- Educational credibility is the hook, not the transaction
- CTA closes a knowledge gap, not a sale

**8-step email copy process:**
1. Lock the angle (compound education, stack rationale, or protocol context)
2. Answer "so what" before the first word
3. Lead with the insight, not the setup
4. One job per paragraph
5. Cut to 60–80 words in the body
6. CTA closes the knowledge gap, not the transaction
7. Compliance check (see `compliance.md`)
8. Read it as Chase — does it feel like something he would actually send?

## Steve-to-Chase Communication Style

When Steve is writing *to* Chase (updates, briefs, recommendations):

- Direct and scannable
- Section headers, plain bullet points, data-forward
- Professionally warm — not stiff, not overly casual
- No dense paragraphs
- No filler closers ("Let me know if you have questions!", "Happy to chat!")
- No em dashes
- Lead with the recommendation, then the rationale
